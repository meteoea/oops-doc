Detailed Description

template<typename MODEL>
class oops::Departures< MODEL >

Difference between two observation vectors.

A departure is the difference between two observations. The archetypal example is

\[ d = y - {\cal G}(x) = \begin{bmatrix} \bar{y}_1 \\ .. \\ \bar{y}_L \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} \bar{\cal G}_1 && .. && .. \\ .. && .. && .. \\ .. && .. && \bar{\cal G}_L \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \bar{x}_1 \\ .. \\ \bar{x}_L \end{bmatrix} \]

where

\[ \bar{y}_j = \begin{bmatrix} \tilde{y}_j \\ \tilde{b}_j \\ \end{bmatrix} \]

and

\[ \bar{\cal G}_j = \begin{bmatrix} \tilde{\cal G}_j && \tilde{\cal P}_j \\ 0 && \tilde{\cal P}_j \end{bmatrix} \]

\[ \bar{x}_j = \begin{bmatrix} x \\ \beta_j \end{bmatrix} \]

and \( L \) is the number of ObservationSpace objects held by ObservationSpaces, \( \bar{y}_j \) is implemented by ControlObsVector and has two components: observation \( \tilde{y}_j \) and bias \( \tilde{b}_j \) vectors (each implemented by ObsVector).

Departures hold a vector of ControlObsVector objects.

Keeping an observation space vector here is necessary for the implementation of generic observation error covariance matrices.

Refer to the documentation of CostJo to better understand the structure.
